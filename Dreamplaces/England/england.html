England

By Vince Harvey E. Bobiles | 1st of February, Year 2026

Why I Want to Visit England

England is one of the places I want to visit because of its deep history, iconic landmarks, and cultural influence. I would love to see London in person, visit historical locations like the Tower of London, and walk through museums that preserve centuries of world history. Beyond London, I am also interested in seeing countryside towns, old castles, and universities known around the world. It feels like a destination where modern life and historical identity exist side by side.

Quick Facts

England is part of the United Kingdom and has a long influence on language, literature, music, and governance. Its capital city, London, is one of the most visited cities in the world. The country has four clear seasons and is known for landmarks like Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, and Stonehenge. England is also home to famous football clubs, historic universities, and a wide variety of local traditions.

English is one of the most spoken languages globally, and many world-famous writers, scientists, and artists came from England. This blend of history and modern influence is one reason it remains a top travel destination.

England also has a strong tradition of arts, theater, and music, from Shakespeare's legacy to modern bands that shaped global pop culture. Its combination of old institutions and modern creativity makes it an engaging destination for both history lovers and younger travelers.

What I Want to Experience

I want to experience walking along the River Thames, exploring museums like the British Museum, and seeing the architecture of places I have only seen in photos and films. I also want to visit a small English town and feel the slower pace compared to major cities.

Another experience I want is to watch a live football match and feel the atmosphere in the stadium. I also want to try classic food, ride local trains, and observe the everyday culture up close.

I would also like to visit famous libraries and university towns like Oxford or Cambridge, then compare that calm academic atmosphere with the fast pace of London. Experiencing both sides of England is something I really look forward to.
