Mount Everest

By Vince Harvey E. Bobiles | 1st of February, Year 2026

Why I Want to Visit Mount Everest

Mount Everest represents challenge, discipline, and the desire to push human limits. I have this desire to climb to the summit of Everest even though I know it is a dangerous endeavor and probably my last trip to this Earth, but if it is impossible, visiting the Everest region would already be an unforgettable experience. I want to witness the Himalayas, meet local communities, and see the mountain that has inspired climbers for generations. The scale of Everest reminds people how powerful nature is and how small we are compared to it.

Quick Facts

Mount Everest is the highest mountain above sea level, standing at over 8,800 meters. It is located on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China. The mountain is called Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet. Climbers usually approach Everest from either the Nepal side or the Tibet side, and both routes require serious preparation because of extreme weather and high-altitude conditions.

The climbing season is short, and altitude sickness is a major risk even for experienced climbers. This is why preparation, acclimatization, and weather timing are critical in the Everest region. Despite the danger, Everest remains a symbol of determination and endurance. Every year, people from many countries train for months just to reach the base camp area and experience the mountain's scale firsthand. And another fact is that most deaths occur on the death zone, the area above 8,000 meters.

And do you know the most interesting thing about Everest? The summit of the mountain is made of fossiliferous limestone which means that once upon a time, this mountain was under the sea.

What I Want to Experience

I want to experience that breathlessness, the feeling of my heart that is about to burst out of my chest being so high up that you can clearly see the stars in the sky. That this right here below my feet is the highest place I have ever been to on this Earth. Will the emptiness of my heart be filled with the brightness and whiteness of the snow that surrounds me? Will the cold convince me that I am really alive? How hard would I fight to stay alive to reach the peak of this mountain and climb back down? Will I even continue to climb even if I don't feel my legs anymore? What things will I miss the most about this Earth that will try to convince me to turn back? How much will I cry? How nervous would I be? How scared? I wonder how alive I would feel.

Above it all, I wonder how many questions will I ask myself? How many answers will I find? Maybe it is ironic, that sometimes we feel most alive the closer we are to the gates of death. Yes, sometimes... to feel alive, we need to get ourselves closer to death. Happiness is on the other side of fear, a very wise person once said. And I totally believe that.
